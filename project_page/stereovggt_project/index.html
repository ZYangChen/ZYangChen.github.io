Abstract

With the advancement of 3D devices, the requirements for stereo vision tasks, including stereo matching and stereo conversion, have emerged as a significant research area. Current stereo vision backbones typically leverage monocular depth estimation (MDE) models or visual foundation models (VFMs). However, these backbones are predominantly pretrained without explicit supervision of camera poses, resulting in a deficiency in camera-awareness that inherently constrains binocular perception. Motivated by this limitation, we investigate the potential of Visual Geometry Grounded Transformer (VGGT), a feed-forward model trained with camera poses and 3D priors, as a feature backbone. Nevertheless, its direct application to stereo vision yields sub-optimal performance. We observe that VGGT features exhibit more pronounced degradation compared to VFMs and MDE models, presenting a barrier to its utility as a reliable backbone for stereo vision tasks. To bridge this gap, we propose StereoVGGT, a feature backbone specifically tailored for stereo vision. By re-weighting the original VGGT and introducing a novel, training-free feature adjustment pipeline, we mitigate degradation and unlock the latent camera calibration knowledge embedded within the model. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our StereoVGGT-based binocular matching network ranks 1st among all published methods on the KITTI online benchmark. Furthermore, our stereo conversion framework achieves SOTA performance, validating that StereoVGGT serves as a highly effective backbone that advances the field of stereo vision.

StereoVGGT integrates the camera geometry knowledge encoded in VGGT while preserving robust feature representation capabilities. It can thus function as a backbone for stereo vision.
